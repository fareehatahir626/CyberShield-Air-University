ATTACK TYPES

Know Your Enemy

Click on any attack type to learn more about how it works and how to defend against it.

Phishing

Deceptive emails that steal your credentials

Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers impersonate trusted entities via email, SMS, or fake websites to steal passwords, credit card numbers, and personal data.

High Risk Email Social Engineering
Always verify sender email addresses before clicking any link.
Learn More

Malware

Malicious software that damages systems

Malware (malicious software) includes viruses, worms, trojans, spyware, and adware. It can steal data, corrupt files, spy on your activities, or give attackers remote control.

Critical Software System
Keep antivirus updated and avoid downloading from untrusted sources.
Learn More

Ransomware

Encrypts your files and demands payment

Ransomware encrypts all your files and demands a ransom (usually in cryptocurrency) to restore access. Even after payment, recovery is not guaranteed.

Severe Encryption Financial
Regular offline backups are the best defense against ransomware.
Learn More

DDoS Attack

Overwhelms servers with massive traffic

Distributed Denial of Service attacks flood a server or network with traffic from thousands of compromised devices (botnets), making services unavailable to legitimate users.

High Risk Network Availability
Use DDoS protection services and traffic filtering solutions.
Learn More

Man-in-the-Middle

Intercepts communication between two parties

MITM attacks intercept and possibly alter communications between two parties who believe they're communicating directly. Common on unsecured public WiFi networks.

High Risk Network Interception
Use HTTPS and a VPN on public networks.
Learn More

Social Engineering

Manipulates people to reveal information

Social engineering exploits human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities. Attackers manipulate people into giving up passwords, clicking links, or granting access through trust.

Critical Human Manipulation
Always verify identities before sharing any sensitive information.
Learn More
STAY SAFE

How to Protect Yourself

Essential practices everyone should follow to stay safe in the digital world.

Strong Passwords

Use long, complex passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager and never reuse passwords.

  • Minimum 12 characters
  • Use a password manager
  • Never reuse passwords

Enable 2FA

Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security beyond just your password. Enable it on all important accounts.

  • Use authenticator apps
  • Enable on email & banking
  • Backup recovery codes

Keep Software Updated

Software updates patch security vulnerabilities. Keeping your OS, browser, and apps updated is critical to your protection.

  • Enable automatic updates
  • Update all apps regularly
  • Patch promptly

Safe Browsing

Be cautious on public WiFi. Always look for HTTPS, use a VPN on public networks, and avoid accessing sensitive accounts.

  • Use a VPN
  • Check for HTTPS
  • Avoid public WiFi for banking

Backup Your Data

Regular backups protect you from ransomware and data loss. Follow the 3-2-1 rule for maximum protection.

  • 3 copies of data
  • 2 different media types
  • 1 offsite/offline backup

Stay Aware

Most attacks exploit human error. Stay educated, think before you click, and always verify unexpected requests for information.

  • Verify before you trust
  • Think before you click
  • Report suspicious activity
